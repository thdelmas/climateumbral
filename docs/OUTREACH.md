## 10 · OSM community post (community.openstreetmap.org, English)

**Title:** Your air_conditioning=yes tags now guide people to cool
rooms during heatwaves

Hi all — climateumbral.eu is an open-source map of Europe's
official climate shelters (Barcelona, Paris, Vienna, Lyon — from
municipal open data) with a second tier built entirely on OSM:
places tagged `air_conditioning=yes` (plus `shop=mall`) appear as
"air-conditioned public places" — clearly labeled as
community-reported, not city-verified — in a one-tap finder for
people who are too hot right now (climateumbral.eu/#cool, five
languages, no app).

Practical upshot: every `air_conditioning=yes` you add to a
library, pharmacy, café or supermarket makes a real difference in
your city during the next heatwave — some cities render dozens of
places, others almost none, purely reflecting tag coverage.
Attribution is shown (© OpenStreetMap contributors, ODbL), queries
are polite (bbox-first, day-long tile caching, instance failover).
Feedback and issues welcome:
github.com/thdelmas/climateumbral.

## Sending notes

- Verify each recipient address on the portal's own contact page
  before sending; none are listed here by design.
- Send city emails on a hot-forecast week if possible — inboxes
  care more.
- After each send, watch https://climateumbral.eu/api/stats for
  the matching page_{city} counter.
- Every email now asks for feedback — treat any reply, even a
  correction, as the win: a city that corrects one shelter's hours
  has adopted the map. Corrections land fastest filed as GitHub
  issues with the city named.
