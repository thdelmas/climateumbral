ClimateUmbral — press kit

The map of official climate shelters and cool places across Europe — with a one-tap finder for people who are too hot right now, in five languages. Free, open source, no accounts, no tracking.

The one-paragraph version

Heat killed an estimated 61,700 people in Europe in the summer of 2022 (Ballester et al., Nature Medicine). Cities respond with climate-shelter networks — cooled public rooms — but each city publishes its list separately, if at all. ClimateUmbral aggregates them onto one map: 939 official climate shelters from Barcelona, Paris, Vienna and Grand Lyon (from each city's own open data, refreshed continuously), plus air-conditioned public places across all of Europe from OpenStreetMap. A panic-simple finder at climateumbral.eu/#cool locates the nearest cool room in one tap, shows opening hours and walking directions, and speaks English, Spanish, Catalan, French and German.

Verifiable facts

939 official climate shelters on the map, from four municipal open datasets: Barcelona (543, Xarxa de refugis climàtics), Paris (279, Îlots de fraîcheur), Vienna (36, Coole Zonen), Grand Lyon (81, équipements publics climatisés).
Europe-wide second tier: air-conditioned public places (malls, libraries, pharmacies, cafés) from OpenStreetMap's air_conditioning tag — verified rendering in 16 cities from Sevilla to Warsaw, including cities with no official shelter list.
Zero data custody: no accounts, no cookies-for-tracking, geolocation never leaves the browser. Usage statistics are bare public counters: /api/stats.
Open: Apache-2.0 source at github.com/thdelmas/climateumbral; every data source attributed and documented, including a guide for cities that want their network added.

The underlying science

  • Ballester et al., Nature Medicine 2023 — 61,672 heat-related deaths in Europe, summer 2022.
  • Iungman et al., The Lancet 2023 — over 4% of summer deaths in 93 European cities attributable to urban heat islands; 30% tree cover could prevent about a third.
  • Ballester et al., Nature Medicine 2024 — adaptation measurably reduces deaths at equal temperatures.

Story angles

  • Service: "Where to cool down in [city] this week" — the finder answers it for any European city, with hours.
  • Accountability: which cities publish their shelter networks as open data — and which don't (most don't).
  • Crowd: every OpenStreetMap contributor who tags a cool café improves the map for their whole city.
